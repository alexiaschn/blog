# Current research interests

## Context 

An ecosystem in 3 parts :

1. Writing and editing -> text editors : Authors, Editors of journals
2. Publishing and indexing -> diffuseurs (journal providers), search engines : Developpers, Ontologists
3. Querying et Retrieving -> search engines : Developpers, Researchers, Students

How is the research ecosystem influenced by inductive algorithms and current AI tools in the pipeline of production-to-query-of-articles ?

## Some issues with LLM-based apps and functionalities

1. Writing and editing: standardisation of language and ideasé 
2. Publishing and indexing: citation concentration [@desollapriceLittleScienceBig1963; @mertonMatthewEffectScience1968], black box algorithms.
3. Querying and retrieving: confirmation bias, filter bubbles [@pariserFilterBubbleWhat2011]

Bonus: data sovereignty, uncertain foundation of science on good-faith. 

**New biases or exercerbation of already existing dynamics?**  


## A Paradigmatic issue 

LLMs are based on the distributional hypothesis [@harrisDistributionalStructure1981]: 

> You shall know a word by the company it keeps. 
> --- @firthStudiesLinguisticAnalysis1962

vs. for instance generative linguistics: 

> Colorless green ideas sleep furiously
> --- @chomskySyntacticStructures1957

In the context of writing and information retrieval: **is the most probable output the most relevant output?**

## Discoverability/Découvrabilité 


**Findability/Trouvabilité**

**Serendipity/Trouvaille** : 'browsing' is a 4 dimensions process [@riceResultsMotivatingThemes2001] : 
1. the act of scanning;
2. the presence or absence of purpose;
3. the specificity of search outcomes or goals; 
4. and knowledge about the resource and object sought.


![Modelisation of serendipity with reflexive intent [@makriComingInformationSerendipitously2012]](img/model.png)


Serendipity is a process rather than a goal, and this process implies necessarily a selection step based on complex cognitive connexions between past and present information. 

Putting serendipity at the forefront of dedicated research and writing tools can be a way to 1. reconnect digital tools and the internet to its roots (open exploration based on a network of unrelated data) 2. allow human agency to be exercised and a support for ai litteracy. 

## Main goals

- Expliciting the biases, improving on AI- and digital- literacy.
- Suggesting epistemic alternatives : e.g. valorisation of serendipity and corrections as an engagement strategy vs. personalization of interactions. 
- Developping tools and functionnality materialising this theoretical standpoint : 
    1. Semantic enriching with LOD embedded in the text : text editor Stylo 
    2. Alternative exploration of large corpora on qualitative aspects (e.g. citation dis/agreement) : journals, érudit, OpenEdition, Isidore.
    3. Querying strategy using semantic structured data (via IEML) as opposed to natural language : Isidore.
